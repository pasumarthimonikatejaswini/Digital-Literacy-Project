# Case Study: Phishing Attack on College Students

What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or UPI credentials by pretending to be a trusted entity.

How it Typically Happens ?
1. The attacker sends a fake email or message that looks like it comes from a bank, university, or payment app.  
2. The message contains urgent language like “Your account will be blocked” or "Verify your UPI ID now". 
3. A link in the message leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one.  
4. The victim enters their login or payment details.  
5. The attacker captures this information and uses it to steal money or identity.

Who is Targeted?
- College students who are new to online banking and UPI apps.  
- Individuals who are less cautious about verifying links.  

Consequences:  
Victims may lose money directly from their bank accounts, suffer identity theft, or face emotional stress.
In one real case reported in India , students lost thousands of rupees after entering details on a fake scholarship portal.
